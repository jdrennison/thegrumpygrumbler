South East MEP Sally Hadland described the relationship between Sunisa Suriyamongkol, a UKIP-supporting mobile food seller in Ramsgate, Kent, and her husband Bradley Stone in "offensive" terms.

"I mean, look at their age difference. No fucking way she wants him for love and he's defo had her flown in."

Bradley Stone said it was outrageous, rude and offensive and he and his wife would withdraw their UKIP membership. Ms Hadland has issued an apology.

The MEP said: "Jesus Christ, here we go again. I'm sorry that my comments were overheard and brought to the couple's attention."

"It was poor judgement that I had not chosen a more private location to express my views. My constituents weren't supposed to hear that I think British women are too good for this old, fat balding chap or that his wife had found an easy ticket to escape poverty and a military regime."

She was interviewed by the Bed With Two Wrong Sides Broadcaster and had agreed to be filmed while she met local people and UKIP members in Ramsgate.

She wore a microphone and was aware she was being recorded.

Mr Stone and Mrs Suriyamongkol have met Ms Hadland at UKIP rallies.

Mrs Suriyamongkol said: "I'm from Thailand. I'm married to an Englishman whom I love and now I'm British."

"What should I do to prove I love Bradley? Return the jewellery and the Merc I've got from him?"

Mr Stone said Ms Hadland's comments were "unacceptable".

"I'm deeply in love with Sunisa and we're very happy together. I hear her often giggle on the phone and sometimes she says 'not yet, he's still here but he's going on a business trip next week.'. I guess she wants to have fun with her girlfriends and sometimes she may think I'm in the way."

"I totally respect that."
