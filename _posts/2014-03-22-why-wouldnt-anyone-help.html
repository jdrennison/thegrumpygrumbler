Oh. Right.

Even if you are some spec-ops veteran or a martial arts expert and could take him, we live in a society where if you're not paid handle such incidents, the cops won't take kindly to you getting involved in a potential fight. Be that as it may, even people who are trained to knock out others with bare hands cannot know the outcome of a fight beforehand and could still be sent to hospital. Not defending themselves or a loved one but a complete stranger. Most people (men included) are not trained fighters.

I wouldn't intervene if a stranger was getting beaten because:
1. You could get harmed or killed when you could just not have provoked the assailant. Someone else was the object of their wrath. C'est la vie. The two links above are an example of what could happen.
2. You become the new target giving the original target the opportunity to scarper. Would the original target join back the fight?
3. We live in a society where this can get you arrested if you're not trained and appointed by the state to break up fights. When you are arrested:

  • You have to find and pay for a good solicitor
  • You have to take time off work
  • You, your friends and family are put through trauma for days, weeks or months.
  • If you're not trained and manage subdue the assailant you don't know what amount of force could be regarded reasonable by the police and if they think you used excessive force you could be charged.

All this not when you've done something wrong but something very right.

At least this is what the media says can happen and just who am I to dispute a story saying "Man held by police for defending his home from burglars".

Would you like to have one or more of these happen to you over an incident involving a total stranger? I wouldn't. As an eye witness I'd give a statement to the police when they arrived on scene but never intervene.
